The Problems We Are Solving

Sound Familiar?

These are the pain points we have experienced firsthand after decades of self-managing rental properties. We are building APM to address them — but we need to know if your experience matches ours.

01

QuickBooks Was Not Built for Landlords

You are paying for software that does not understand properties, leases, or Schedule E. You spend hours categorizing expenses that should be automatic. Setting up classes for each property is a nightmare.

02

Enterprise Tools Are Overkill

AppFolio and Buildium charge $298+ minimums with per-unit fees designed for professional PM companies managing hundreds of units. You do not need a leasing CRM — you need to know if your properties are making money.

03

Flying Blind Until Tax Season

You send receipts to your accountant and find out in April whether you made or lost money. No monthly visibility, no per-property performance tracking, no ability to make decisions in real time.

What We Are Exploring

Features Under Consideration

These are the capabilities we are evaluating for APM. Some are based on our own workflows, others on early conversations with landlords and accountants. Your feedback determines what gets built first.

01

AI-Powered Bank Transaction Import

Upload your bank CSV and let AI categorize transactions automatically. The system learns your patterns over time — recurring vendors, property assignments, expense categories.

Based on our workflow
02

Receipt Capture with OCR

Snap a photo of any receipt from your phone. AI extracts vendor, amount, and date, suggests the property and expense category. One tap to confirm. Receipt image stored for audit trail.

Based on our workflow
03

Monthly Financial Reports

P&L statements, balance sheets, cash flow reports, property profitability analysis, mortgage and insurance tracking, depreciation schedules, rent roll, and Schedule E — generated automatically.

Based on our workflow
04

Multiple LLCs, One Dashboard

Real double-entry accounting with multi-entity support. Each LLC gets its own chart of accounts, bank mappings, and reports. See consolidated or per-entity views. No cross-contamination.

Based on our workflow
05

Year-End CPA Package

One click generates everything your accountant needs: P&L by entity, balance sheet, depreciation schedule, transaction ledger, receipt backup, and Schedule E mapping. CPA portal access included so your accountant can pull reports directly.

Based on our workflow
06

QuickBooks Data Import

Export your QuickBooks file, upload it, and your transaction history, chart of accounts, and vendor list migrate over. No starting from zero. No losing years of financial data to switch platforms.

Exploring
07

Tenant Communication and Work Orders

Tenants message you through a portal, messaging app integration (Telegram, WhatsApp), or text — and a pending work order is created automatically. Track maintenance from request to contractor payment with full cost history per property.

Exploring
08

Tenant Portal

A simple page where tenants can submit maintenance requests, message their landlord, view payment history, and access lease information. Integrates with Zillow, Rentler, or whatever rent collection method you already use.

Why We Are Building This
"We have been self-managing single-family rentals for decades. We tried every tool out there. Nothing worked the way landlords actually think."

After years of fighting QuickBooks, cobbling together spreadsheets, and paying accountants to organize our own mess, we started building the system we actually needed. It began as a dashboard for managing our own SFR portfolio across multiple LLCs in the Virginia Beach market — and it proved that a better approach was possible.

Now we are aiming to build the app that works for every self-managing landlord — based on proven workflows and our own journey through the property management cycle. But we are not building in a vacuum. We want to hear from you first.

What are your biggest pain points? What reports do you actually need? What would make you switch from what you are using now? Your feedback shapes what we build.
